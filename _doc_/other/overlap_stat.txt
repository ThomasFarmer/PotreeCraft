How to read the output
Main fields

front_best
Best overlap percent with a likely forward/back neighbor.

side_best
Best overlap percent with a likely left/right neighbor.

risk_class
Simple classification:

good → front ≥ 80 and side ≥ 65
usable → front ≥ 70 and side ≥ 55
weak → front ≥ 60 and side ≥ 40
poor → below that

These are not universal standards, but they are reasonable screening thresholds.

Important caveats

This script is still an estimate, because it infers flight direction from the geometry and neighbor layout rather than using a full mission plan or exact image corner projection.

It works best when:

- the footprints are reasonably oriented
- the dataset has some flight-line structure
- duplicates are removed

It is much more meaningful than the earlier “overlap with all neighbors” metric when your goal is to compare with frontlap / sidelap expectations.

What result would indicate a weak survey

Roughly:

front_best < 75% often → weak front overlap
side_best < 60% often → weak side overlap
many weak or poor rows → manual / irregular acquisition likely not ideal for measurement
